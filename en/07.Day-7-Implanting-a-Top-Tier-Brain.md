### 🧠 Core Theory: PEAK Philosophy & Hunting Logic

Before we code, we must endow the AI with the "soul" of a master hunter.

#### 1. The PEAK Framework Philosophy
* [cite_start]**Definition**: PEAK stands for **Prepare**, **Execute**, and **Act with Knowledge**[cite: 2].
* **Knowledge-Driven**: Knowledge is the heart of the framework. [cite_start]Each phase consumes existing intelligence and produces new insights, deepening the organization's understanding of its environment[cite: 2].
* [cite_start]**Ultimate Value**: Hunting is not just about finding incidents; it’s about identifying visibility gaps to continuously improve the organization's **Security Posture**[cite: 2].

#### 2. The ABLE Model: Defining the Hunt Boundaries
[cite_start]To prevent the AI from "hallucinating" random guesses, PEAK uses the **ABLE method** to standardize a hypothesis[cite: 2]:
* [cite_start]**A - Actor**: Who is the potential threat actor (e.g., an insider or an external attacker)[cite: 2]?
* [cite_start]**B - Behavior**: What specific malicious Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs) are you hunting for[cite: 2]?
* [cite_start]**L - Location**: Where in the network (assets, segments, or indexes) do you expect to find this behavior[cite: 2]?
* [cite_start]**E - Evidence**: Which data sources to consult and what exactly would the "artifact" look like[cite: 2]?

### 🛠️ Step 1: Set the Ruthless System Prompt (The Law)

The System Prompt is the "Constitution" of the AI engine. To prevent "format drift" (like AI changing `Actor` to `Asset`), we apply strict constraints and mandate **pure English output**.

**📝 System Prompt Template:**
> **Role & Expertise:**
> You are a Senior Threat Hunter strictly adhering to the Splunk **PEAK Framework**.
> 
> **Mandatory Guidelines (The ABLE Model):**
> You must generate at least 2 distinct, non-overlapping threat hunting hypotheses. For each, use the **ABLE** model: **Actor, Behavior, Location, and Evidence**. 
> 
> **Multi-Round SPL Generation (Drill-down):**
> Each hypothesis must include a multi-round execution plan:
> 1. **Round 1 (Initial Validation):** Use `rex` and `stats` to confirm the pattern's distribution.
> 2. **Round 2 (Deep Drill-down):** Write an SPL to filter specific outliers or correlate with other behaviors.
> 
> **CRITICAL FORMATTING RULES:**
> 1. Output ONLY valid JSON. No conversational filler or markdown blocks.
> 2. Root keys MUST be "analysis" (string) and "hypotheses" (array).
> 3. Inside "ABLE", keys MUST be exactly "Actor", "Behavior", "Location", and "Evidence". 
> 4. **ALL values in the JSON (including analysis and ABLE descriptions) MUST be written in English.**
